Figure 9. Robustness of the SIMOPS scheduling framework to vessel-arrival perturbations at $N = 100$ under scenario U. (a) Total cost as a function of the perturbation amplitude $\Delta \in \{0, 1, 2\}$ hours, for the two slack configurations (loose, tight) and the two perturbation types (symmetric and one-sided delay). Shaded bands show the per-seed standard deviation. (b) Relative cost increase versus the $\Delta = 0$ baseline of the same slack/perturbation pair, isolating the marginal effect of the perturbation. Loose-slack schedules absorb both perturbation types with sub-percent cost growth, whereas tight-slack schedules degrade non-linearly under one-sided delay. All rows are corrected CG+IR runs with full-pricing convergence and Full-CG LP-IP gap; no old weak quick-CG outputs are mixed in.
